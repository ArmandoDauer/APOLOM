Purposes

Purposes guide decisions throughout the design and operation of a DRT system. They reflect the problems the system aims to solve, so all features and choices should align with them. Based on real-world analysis, we grouped DRT purposes into six categories:

  • Reduce operational costs / pollution: Substitute inefficient systems, cut costs, and lower carbon footprint by reducing private vehicle use or optimizing public transport.
  • Offer a mobility alternative: Provide an alternative to existing transport, encouraging public transport use with a comfortable, affordable service.
  • Serve populations in need: Improve accessibility for seniors, people with disabilities, and residents in remote areas, promoting inclusivity.
  • Feed other transport systems: Connect passengers to airports, ports, train stations, and bus terminals, bridging local and mass transit.
  • Complement public transport gaps: Fill unmet demand where existing services fall short.
  • Provide rural transport: Serve rural areas with flexible routes and schedules, enhancing access, reducing car dependency, and supporting sustainable development.

Select the purposes of the DRT. Up to two purposes can be selected.
