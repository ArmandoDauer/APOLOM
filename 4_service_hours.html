Service Hours

Service hours define when the DRT operates and are key to meeting its purpose. They can target peak travel, commuter schedules, or improve access for underserved populations. Strategically defined hours can boost efficiency and better serve target passengers.

When resources are limited, service hours can optimize capacity. Instead of continuous coverage, the system may operate in time segments, ensuring coverage across the area while balancing demand and resources.

Common service hour configurations identified in real-world cases include:

  • Conventional Business Hours: Matches standard public transport schedules, complementing existing services or improving network access.
  • Off-Peak Hours Only: Operates when demand is low, supplementing or replacing fixed-route services.
  • Peak Hours Only: Focuses on high-demand periods to relieve pressure on regular transport or support standalone operations.
  • Specific Time Windows: Operates during defined periods based on demand, resources, or other transport availability.
  • Night Services: Provides mobility during nighttime when conventional transport is limited or unavailable.
