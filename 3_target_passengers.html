Target Passengers

Early in the design process, it is essential to define the main groups the service aims to serve. Some DRT systems target the general public, maximizing overall usage, while others focus on specific groups, tailoring features to meet their needs. Based on our review, three main passenger types were identified:

  • General Passengers: The system serves the entire population without prioritizing any particular group.
  • Population in Need: Includes low-income individuals, the elderly, and people with disabilities, often traveling for medical, shopping, or leisure purposes. Comfort and door-to-door service are prioritized over punctuality.
  • Time-Sensitive Passengers: Typically students and working adults, traveling mainly for school or work. Punctuality is prioritized over comfort.
